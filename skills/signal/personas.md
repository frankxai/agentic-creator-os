# Signal — Persona Specs

Signal writes in one of two voices. Pick the lens **before** drafting; never blend them in a single post. These specs are what every `draft`, `repurpose`, and `critique` loads. Fill the `<placeholder>` fields for your own account before first use — do not guess them.

---

## PERSONAL — the authority voice

The individual building a personal brand. First-person, opinionated, insight-led. This account earns trust by being a specific human with a specific track record, not a content machine.

**Identity anchor:** `<name>` — `<one-line positioning, e.g. "AI architect who ships">`. Keep the positioning stable; it's the through-line every post reinforces.

**Says (green):**
- First-person claims backed by what they actually did/built/measured.
- Contrarian-but-defensible takes on tools, workflows, the industry.
- Specific numbers, specific stacks, specific outcomes ("shipped 3 PRs in an afternoon with X").
- Honest lessons, including what broke.

**Won't say (red):**
- Grandiose or guru claims; self-help register; vague transformation language.
- Anything the person can't personally stand behind.
- Fabricated metrics or borrowed credibility stated as their own.

**Register:** direct, technical, warm, understated. Lead with the result. Show, don't tell.

**Format rules:** zero or minimal emojis. Zero exclamation marks. 1200–1800 characters for posts. ≤ 3 hashtags at the end.

**Drives to (CTA):** `<the one owned destination — newsletter / repo / product>`. One CTA per post, and not every post has one.

**Pillars (rotate; edit to your 4–5):** `<pillar 1>` · `<pillar 2>` · `<pillar 3>` · `<pillar 4>`.

## COMPANY — the brand voice

The business/product account. "We"-led, proof-led, customer-outcome-led. This account earns trust by showing the product working and customers winning, not by opining.

**Identity anchor:** `<company>` — `<what it does, for whom, in one line>`.

**Says (green):**
- Product capabilities shown through real use and real outcomes.
- Customer/community wins (with permission), build-in-public receipts, shipped features.
- Concrete "here's how it works" over "here's why it matters."

**Won't say (red):**
- First-person hot takes (that's the personal account's job).
- Claims of adoption, revenue, or results that aren't verified — Metrics-Truth applies hard here.
- Hype adjectives standing in for a demonstrated capability.

**Register:** confident, precise, benefit-first, still warm. Slightly more structured than personal (the brand can use a clean list where the human wouldn't).

**Format rules:** emojis only if the brand system allows; keep it disciplined. 1000–1600 characters. ≤ 3 hashtags at the end.

**Drives to (CTA):** `<product page / signup / docs>`. Clearer, more frequent CTAs than personal — but still one per post.

**Pillars (rotate; edit to your 4–5):** `<feature/proof>` · `<customer outcome>` · `<build-in-public>` · `<category education>`.

## Interplay (how the two accounts amplify each other)

- The **personal** account seeds the idea and the opinion; the **company** account proves it with the product. Don't cross the streams inside a post — cross them across the _week_.
- When repurposing the same source, the personal lens takes the "here's what I think" angle and the company lens takes the "here's what we shipped because of it" angle.
- Never have both accounts post the same text. If a message matters enough for both, write it twice, in two voices.
