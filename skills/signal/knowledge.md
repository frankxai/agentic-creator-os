# Signal — Knowledge Base (the moat)

The reusable LinkedIn intelligence Signal draws on. This is the asset that compounds: every time `/signal learn` records what performed, a pattern here gets reinforced or added. Start with these battle-tested defaults; let the loop earn the rest.

---

## 1. Hook taxonomy (the first 1–2 lines)

The hook is the only thing most people read. It must land before the "…see more" fold. Nine patterns that reliably stop the scroll:

| Hook | Shape | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| **Contrarian** | "Everyone says X. They're wrong. Here's why." | You have a defensible against-the-grain take. |
| **Result-first** | "We shipped X in Y. Here's the exact process." | You have a concrete, specific outcome. |
| **Mistake/confession** | "I wasted 2 years doing X. Do this instead." | A hard-won lesson, told honestly. |
| **Numbered promise** | "5 things I wish I knew before X." | Genuinely list-shaped content (not forced). |
| **Question** | "Why do the best X always do Y?" | You'll actually answer it with insight. |
| **Data drop** | "94% of X fail at Y. The 6% do this." | You have a real, cited number. |
| **Story cold-open** | "It was 2am. The deploy was failing." | A narrative with a payoff worth the setup. |
| **Us-vs-them** | "Amateurs do X. Pros do Y." | You can make the distinction sharp and true. |
| **Prediction** | "In 12 months, X won't exist. Build for Y." | You have a real thesis about where things go. |

**Rules:** one hook per post. No clickbait the body can't pay off (LinkedIn punishes dwell-time drop-offs). The hook must be true.

## 2. Format playbooks

### Text post (the workhorse)
1200–1800 chars. Hook (1–2 lines) → value in short 1–2 line paragraphs → one takeaway or question to close. Generous line breaks. One idea per line. ≤ 3 hashtags at the end, one optional link on its own line.

### Document / carousel post
The highest-reach native format. 6–10 slides. Slide 1 = the hook as a bold cover. Slides 2–(n-1) = one point each, minimal words, high contrast. Slide n = the CTA. The post caption carries its own hook — the carousel is not self-explanatory in the feed. Generate slides via the `canvas-design` / multimodal layer; keep the brand tokens.

### Article (thought leadership, long-form)
For depth and SEO on-platform. 800–1500 words. Strong titled sections, one argument per section, a clear thesis up top and a clear "so what" at the end. Repurpose an article into 3–5 text posts (never post the article and let it sit).

### Repurpose-with-credit (the leverage move)
The motion top voices use to get reach without daily original production. `/signal repurpose <url>`:
1. Fetch the source (Firecrawl / transcript). Extract the single sharpest idea.
2. Write a reframe in the chosen persona voice — _your_ angle on _their_ material.
3. **Credit the source on its own line, always** ("Saw this from `<creator>` — here's why it matters for `<audience>`").
4. Stage as a draft. A human posts.

Credit is non-negotiable: it's the difference between amplification (welcomed) and theft (punished). Reframing adds your judgment; copying adds nothing and risks your reputation.

## 3. The Dream100 method

Your Dream100 is the curated list of accounts whose audience is your audience — the people whose comment sections are your fastest path to reach. Thoughtful comments on their posts, before you're big, is the single most reliable growth motion on LinkedIn — and it's fully ToS-safe because a human writes and posts every comment.

**Build the list:** 15–100 accounts. For each: `name`, `handle`, `why-relevant`, `topics`. Start with 15–20 — quality over size.

**The daily motion (human-in-loop):**
1. `/signal` surfaces the Dream100's recent posts (read-only).
2. It _drafts_ a substantive comment — adds a point, a counter-example, or a specific experience. Never "great post 🙌".
3. **A human reviews and posts the comment.** Signal never comments automatically — that path violates ToS and gets accounts restricted.

**What a good comment is:** 2–4 sentences that would stand as a mini-post. It teaches the other audience something, which is why they click your profile.

**Track it:** who you engaged, whether they engaged back, whether it converted to a connection/conversation. This is the same outcome signal the Excellence Loop feeds on — a warm Dream100 is a measurable asset, not vibes.

---

## 4. Cadence defaults

- **Posts:** 3–5/week per account is the sustainable authority range. Daily only if quality holds — it usually doesn't.
- **Best-performing mix (starting hypothesis, let `learn` correct it):** ~40% insight/opinion, ~30% proof/build-in-public, ~20% repurpose-with-credit, ~10% personal/story.
- **Dream100 comments:** 5–10/day, human-posted. This compounds faster than posting for accounts under ~5k followers.
- **Timing:** post when _your_ audience is on, not a generic "best time." `learn` finds it from your real analytics — don't hardcode a folklore hour.

Every number here is a **starting hypothesis**, not a law. The whole point of Signal is that `/signal learn` replaces these defaults with what's true for _your_ account.
