# YouTube Short Script Template

## When to Use This Template
- Creating 60-second educational or entertaining content
- Repurposing TikTok/Instagram Reels for YouTube
- Building a YouTube presence with short-form content
- Teaching quick tips or insights in vertical video format
- Maximizing reach across multiple short-form platforms

## Input Fields Needed
- **Core Message**: [The main insight or tip you're sharing]
- **Target Audience**: [Who this is for - be specific]
- **Hook Angle**: [The compelling reason to watch]
- **Visual Elements**: [What viewers will see throughout]
- **Call to Action**: [Subscribe, like, comment, watch more]
- **Value Proposition**: [What they'll learn or gain]

---

## YouTube Shorts vs TikTok/Reels

### Key Differences:
- **Length**: Up to 60 seconds (TikTok/Reels similar)
- **Algorithm**: Prioritizes watch time + subscriber conversion
- **Audience**: Slightly older, more intent-driven
- **CTA**: "Subscribe" is more important than "Follow"
- **Discovery**: Shorts feed + search + suggested videos
- **Monetization**: Shorts Fund + AdSense (if eligible)

### Optimization Strategy:
- First 3 seconds: Hook (same as TikTok)
- Last 5 seconds: Strong subscribe CTA
- Description: Use keywords for search
- Title: Optimize for YouTube search
- Thumbnail: Auto-generated, but first frame matters

## Platform-Specific Formatting

### Video Specs
- **Aspect ratio**: 9:16 (vertical)
- **Resolution**: 1080 x 1920 pixels minimum
- **Length**: Up to 60 seconds (optimal: 30-45 seconds)
- **File format**: MP4, MOV, or WebM
- **File size**: Under 256 GB

### Title Optimization
- **Length**: 40-70 characters (shows fully in feed)
- **Keywords**: Front-load important keywords
- **Clarity**: Make it clear what the video is about
- **Curiosity**: Create interest without clickbait

**Examples:**
- ‚úÖ "How to Grow YouTube Shorts Fast (3 Simple Tips)"
- ‚úÖ "YouTube Shorts Algorithm Explained in 60 Seconds"
- ‚úÖ "I Got 1M Views on My First Short (Here's How)"
- ‚ùå "My YouTube Journey" (too vague)
- ‚ùå "You Won't BELIEVE What Happened!!!" (clickbait)

### Description Optimization
- **First line**: Most important info (shows in feed)
- **Keywords**: Include relevant search terms
- **Hashtags**: #Shorts (required) + 2-3 relevant tags
- **Links**: Add links to longer videos, playlists, or resources
- **Timestamps**: Not needed for Shorts

**Example Description:**
```
3 mistakes that kill YouTube Shorts growth (and how to fix them)

I've grown 3 channels to 100K+ subscribers using these strategies. If you're struggling to get views on Shorts, this will help.

üîó Full YouTube Growth Guide: [link]
üìß Join my newsletter: [link]

#Shorts #YouTubeGrowth #ContentCreator
```

### Captions
- **Auto-captions**: YouTube auto-generates (usually accurate)
- **Editing**: Review and fix any errors
- **Accessibility**: Captions help with watch time
- **SEO**: YouTube indexes caption text for search

## Pro Tips

### First Frame Optimization
Your first frame is your thumbnail:
- Bold, readable text
- High contrast colors
- Your face (if personal brand)
- Clear visual hierarchy
- No clutter

### Pacing
- Fast cuts (every 2-3 seconds)
- No dead air or pauses
- Jump cuts to remove filler words
- B-roll to show what you're explaining
- Text overlays to emphasize key points

### Audio
- Clear voice audio (use external mic if possible)
- Background music (low volume, don't overpower voice)
- Sound effects for emphasis (sparingly)
- Trending audio (less important than TikTok)

### Engagement Signals
YouTube prioritizes:
- Watch time (% of video watched)
- Likes
- Comments
- Shares
- Subscribes from Short
- Click-through to channel

### Posting Strategy
- **Frequency**: 1-3 Shorts per day (consistency matters)
- **Timing**: Less important than TikTok (algorithm-driven)
- **Batching**: Create 7-14 Shorts in one session
- **Variety**: Mix educational, entertaining, and personal

## Advanced Techniques

### The Loop Technique
Make the end connect to the beginning:
- Last frame teases next video
- "Part 2 coming tomorrow"
- "Watch my next Short to see..."
- Creates binge-watching behavior

### The Series Strategy
Create connected Shorts:
- "Day 1 of [challenge]"
- "Part 1: [Topic]"
- "[Number] of [Series]"
- Builds anticipation and loyalty

### The Remix Strategy
Take one long-form video, create 5-10 Shorts:
- Each Short = one key point
- Link back to full video in description
- Drives traffic to long-form content
- Maximizes content ROI

### The Trend Adaptation
Adapt trending formats to your niche:
- "Things I wish I knew about [your topic]"
- "POV: You're learning [your topic]"
- "[Your niche] explained in 60 seconds"
- Leverages trends while staying on-brand

## Repurposing Strategy

### From YouTube Short to Other Platforms:
1. **TikTok**: Upload directly (same video)
2. **Instagram Reels**: Upload directly (same video)
3. **Twitter/X**: Upload as native video
4. **LinkedIn**: Add professional context
5. **Pinterest Idea Pin**: Break into steps
6. **Blog**: Expand into written article
7. **Email**: Embed video in newsletter

### From Long-Form to Shorts:
1. Identify 5-10 key moments
2. Create standalone Short for each
3. Link back to full video in description
4. Use Shorts to drive long-form views

### Variation Strategy:
- Same content, different hook (test 3 versions)
- Same hook, different content (series)
- Longer version for main channel (5-10 minutes)
- Behind-the-scenes of making the Short

## Analytics to Track

### Key Metrics:
- **Views**: Total views (vanity metric)
- **Watch time**: Average % watched (most important)
- **Likes**: Engagement signal
- **Comments**: Engagement quality
- **Shares**: Viral potential
- **Subscribers from Short**: Conversion rate
- **Click-through to channel**: Interest in you

### What to Optimize:
- Low watch time ‚Üí improve hook or shorten video
- Low subscribers ‚Üí strengthen CTA
- Low engagement ‚Üí ask better questions
- Low views ‚Üí optimize title/description for search
- High views, low subscribers ‚Üí improve CTA or content quality
